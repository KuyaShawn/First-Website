Car Care Tips


As you may know, having a car break on you could be very costly. But you can prevent this by maintaining your car on a regular basis and checking your engine for any leaks or cracks. As this will help you and your car in a long run by keeping it in good care. Here, we will show you videos of how to do quick maintenance on your car by yourself.

Oil changes


Keeping up with oil changes is the most important to all of these checkups. as running with old oil can damage the engine in the long run. It is recommended to replace your oil every 5k miles as it will have good long term effects on your engine components and won't cause much damage.

Tramission fluid change


Changing your transmission fluid is also a great idea, as it will help with making your car shift a lot smoother because having old fluid can get dirty with small metal shavings, which isn't good in the long run. And you won't have to worry about changing your transmission for a long time if you do it regularly.

Brake fluid change


Plenty of people forgets to change their brake fluid and changing it is very important as it will help make sure your brakes are working properly, and if you don't change it, Usually the fluid will leave your entire brake system vulnerable to corrosion, which could damage your anti-lock brake system and the metal components. it's recommended that you should change your fluid every 30K miles.

Brake pad & Rotor change


Not only is doing your brake fluid important, It's also recommended to change your brake pads and rotors all together In order to be safe in the streets. So you can brake on time when something happens when you are driving.

Coolant change


Changing your radiator fluid once in a while is fine, as this fluid is the least to be concerned about. But it doesn't mean you shouldn't check if you're low or if turned to another color can sometimes be very common for older cars. Changing the fluid can help the car by keeping it cooler because having old fluid can deteriorate and become more delude.
